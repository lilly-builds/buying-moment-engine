# Loom walkthrough outline

A script skeleton for a short (about 5 to 6 minute) Loom that walks a viewer through the product
and how a team rolls it out. Two audiences in one take: someone deciding whether it is real (the
product tour) and someone who would actually run it (rollout and training). Record it on the live
URL against real, populated data.

**Tone:** plain and confident, like showing a colleague a tool you are proud of. Let the product
carry it. No slides; screen-share the running app.

---

## 0. Cold open (0:00 to 0:20)

- Open straight on the **feed**, already populated with real practices.
- One line: "This is a live feed of healthcare practices hitting a buying moment right now. Each
  one comes with a brief that is ready to work. An AE opens this and starts selling, they do not
  start researching."

## 1. User Story: the AE's morning (0:20 to 1:20)

- Scroll the **feed**. Point out the ranking: hottest on top, sorted by how many signals are firing.
- Read one headline out loud: the buying moment, the signal count, the freshness badge.
- The message: the AE is handed a constant flow, not a blank page.

## 2. The timing thesis (1:20 to 2:10)

- "A cold list tells you who fits the profile. This tells you who is ready to buy, and why now."
- Name the three signals in plain words: they are hiring for the front desk, patients are
  complaining they cannot get through, or the practice just grew.
- One sentence on why timing beats demographics for conversion.

## 3. The brief, and why you can trust it (2:10 to 3:20)

- Open a **brief**. Walk the two tiers: at-a-glance (who to contact, the recommended action, the
  editable outreach sequence) and call prep (profile, EHR, objections, ROI range).
- **Click a citation.** Show the source open. "Every fact is underline-linked to where it came
  from. One click to verify. Nothing here is a guess the AE has to defend."
- Edit one line of the sequence inline to show it is a real workspace, not a static card.

## 4. Proof it is working: the ROI scoreboard (3:20 to 4:10)

- Open the **scoreboard**. Point out meetings booked, cost per meeting, conversion by signal.
- Land the honesty tags: "Every number is tagged **measured** or **modeled**. Measured is real
  activity from the tool. Modeled is an honest projection. We never dress a projection up as a
  measurement."
- Toggle a per-vertical view to show the same numbers slice by specialty.

## 5. Rollout and training (4:10 to 5:30)

This is the RevOps and enablement half. The key idea: **full value before a single key.**

- **The whole team sees value on day one, with zero setup.** The feed, the briefs, the editing,
  the feedback, and the scoreboard all run before anyone connects anything.
- Show the **guided onboarding tour**: the step cards (gradient orb, one-bold-word instruction, a
  context chip) that coach an AE through working their first lead. Note it is skippable and the
  feed works without it.
- Show the **Send handoff**. The send button is a named, routed handoff to the RevOps owner, not a
  dead end. Each rep's tap adds to a visible demand count, so the users pull the connection in.
- Show the **Connections page** (`/integrations`): the one-time admin setup. One OAuth click for
  HubSpot, two pasted keys for the engine. This is the only setup anyone does, and only the admin
  sees it.
- Say the honest part out loud: nothing sends and nothing writes to a live system until the admin
  connects it. The engine only ever touches public business data.

## 6. Close (5:30 to 6:00)

- Back to the feed. "One connection turns on send and CRM tracking for the whole team. Everything
  else already works."
- End on the thesis: sell on the moment, not the list.

---

## Capture checklist (before you hit record)

- The demo data is populated (real practices, real briefs, real signals) so nothing shows an
  empty state.
- You are on the live URL, signed in.
- Pick one brief in advance that has a strong citation to click and a clean contact card.
- The scoreboard has enough events to show real numbers, not all zeros.
- Have the onboarding tour ready to trigger (fresh tour state) so the step cards appear on cue.

# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The person setting up the tool is a revenue leader or founder at a B2B company: a head of sales, a RevOps lead, a founder still running their own pipeline. They are not engineers. They arrive skeptical, short on time, and unwilling to read a manual. Their job on the first run is narrow: get the engine pointed at their business and see something real, fast. The tool itself adapts to any B2B vertical, so the setup experience has to work for a video ad agency, an HR platform, or a logistics SaaS without changing a line of code.

## Product Purpose

Adapt-It turns a buying-moment sales engine into a self-serve product. An AI interviews the business for about two minutes, then rebuilds the whole app around it: its buying-moment signals, its pitch, its proof, its brand. The person walks away with a live feed of prospects and a brief written in their own voice. Success is the first-run moment where a non-technical operator sees the tool speaking their language and thinks "this already knows my business."

## Positioning

The engine adapts to you, not the other way around. Every other sales tool makes you configure it; this one interviews you and configures itself.

## Brand Personality

Warm and sharp. The Adapter talks like a strong operator who understands your business on the first pass, not a chatbot. Spare, not chatty. Confident without hype. Premium in feel: fewer words, more space, considered motion. It never performs helpfulness, never flatters, never pads.

## Anti-references

Not a support-chat widget (no corner bubble, no "How can I help you today"). Not a stack of gray ChatGPT message bubbles. Not a form wearing a chat costume. Not walls of text. And not written in AI-tell language: no "let's dive in," no "seamless," no "unlock," no "great question," no em dashes.

## Design Principles

The AI does the work; the person confirms. Typing is the exception, never the default. Show, do not tell: the app visibly rebuilds itself as the conversation moves, so the value is felt, not described. One thing at a time, with room to breathe. Earned trust: everything the AI proposes is shown plainly and stays editable. Speak like a person, in the user's words, never the system's.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard operable throughout, visible focus states, and full support for reduced motion (every reveal has a crossfade or instant fallback). Color is never the only signal. Body text and placeholders meet WCAG AA contrast.
